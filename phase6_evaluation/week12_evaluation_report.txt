To evaluate whether the AI Tutor improves upon raw GCC messages,
both the original and rewritten messages were scored on four criteria
using a 1–5 scale.

Scoring Rubric (1 = Very Poor, 5 = Excellent):

  Criterion C1: CLARITY
    1 = completely cryptic, full of jargon
    3 = somewhat understandable with prior knowledge
    5 = immediately clear without any background knowledge

  Criterion C2: ACTIONABILITY
    1 = no guidance on what to do
    3 = hints at what to do but vague
    5 = tells exactly what line to change and how

  Criterion C3: ACCURACY
    1 = wrong explanation
    3 = partially correct
    5 = fully correct and precise

  Criterion C4: SECURITY
    1 = fix introduces security risk
    3 = fix is neutral
    5 = fix promotes secure coding

  OVERALL = average of C1 + C2 + C3 + C4

Evaluators: Project team (3 members), one faculty reviewer
